It was really fun!

I just wanted to share my experience attending a conference abroad and how I decided to do it.

It's been three years since I started volunteering to organize PyCon here in the Philippines. Every year, we meet friends from other countries, so I thought — why shouldn't I go to their conference too? Encouraged by the local community and through our self-improvement program called **PythonPH Kaizend**, I decided to register for PyConID 2019.

It was a YOLO move, honestly — that would be my first time on a plane, a 14-hour flight with a layover (excluding delays), and my first time going outside the Philippines. Overwhelming. So the first thing I did was go through the organizer list. Luckily, I saw a familiar name — Doni, a speaker at our PyCon. I emailed him, and he was very accommodating. That lessened my worry.

Fast-forward to pre-PyCon preparation (Nov 22, 2019) — I was invited by Doni to attend their committee meeting. I was hoping I could help, but I didn't make it on time. The work was almost done — swag bags were already packed, they were wrapping up. They were ready!

We met with Jake from PyCharm at the booth area before the conference registration table. He was at our PyCon too! Always good to see familiar faces and catch up. After that, we went straight to the auditorium to listen to the first keynote speaker, Dr. Ir. Inggriani Liem, with her talk "A Journey to be a Good Software Engineer." I enjoyed her talk even though it was in Bahasa. Everyone was laughing and enjoying it.

![Keynote 1]*(broken image — was on Dev.to S3)*

The next keynote was from Fauzan Erich Emmerling, Mobile Product Engineering Lead at Gojek, with his talk "How Python Changed My Life."

This talk made me rethink how I should code — or how I should live, haha. He talked about simplicity, the philosophy behind Python, and the Zen of Python.
