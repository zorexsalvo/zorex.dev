AWS Summit Manila is one leg of the AWS Global Summit that promotes Amazon Web Services. Their goal is to educate both new and existing customers around the world to be more successful with AWS.

### Registration

An hour before the registration desk opened, the venue was already packed. There was already a queue for early birds. I was lucky to be one of the first 100 delegates — **FREE AWS SHIRT, YAY!** AWS Summit Manila definitely received an overwhelming response.

![Registration]*(broken image — was on zorexsalvo.xyz)*

### Freebies & AWS Passport

After registration, we got our summit kit and the free shirt. We also got an AWS Summit Manila Passport — like a stamp album. We had to collect stamps from the booths (sponsors and partners using AWS) and complete the album to redeem prizes.

![Freebies]*(broken image — was on zorexsalvo.xyz)*
![Passport]*(broken image — was on zorexsalvo.xyz)*

### Booth Exhibition

There were a lot of exciting booths: Intel, GitHub, Globe Telecom, Telstra, WingArc, and more. Each had their own product using AWS — software for Big Data, Business Intelligence, AI, Machine Learning. The booths really had a lot to offer.

![Booths]*(broken image — was on zorexsalvo.xyz)*

After the exhibition, Amazon Web Services Managing Director Nick Walton gave his welcome remarks, followed by the opening keynote from Mai-Lan Tomsen Bukovec, VP and GM of Amazon S3, discussing the superpowers AWS has. Local companies that use AWS also shared their migration stories and successes.

### Lunch Time

Not sure if AWS Summit Manila or AWS Food Summit, hahaha. We never got hungry throughout the summit because of the food counters.

![Lunch]*(broken image — was on zorexsalvo.xyz)*

### Breakout Sessions

AWS Summit had two tracks for breakout sessions: Business Transformation and Cloud Innovation — running simultaneously, so we had to pick one. We went with Cloud Innovation since that piqued our interest.

The track started with "Internet of Things with AWS" — a great start. They showcased cool things their platform can do. There were also topics on DevOps, Automation, Application Migration, Serverless Solutions, and Big Data solved using AWS.

### The End

I completed my passport just before the summit ended and went straight to the redemption counter. Sadly, I didn't get the grand prize. What I got instead: a happy tummy, freebies, stickers, knowledge, fun, and the experience from this one-day summit.
